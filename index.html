What is Endodontics?

Endodontics is the branch of dentistry dedicated to the inside of the tooth — diagnosing tooth pain, treating infection, and preserving your natural bite with precision care.

Save Your Tooth

Targeted treatment removes infection while keeping your natural tooth structure whenever possible.

Eliminate Pain

Modern anaesthesia and gentle techniques address the source of discomfort — not just the symptoms.

Long-Term Health

A well-treated tooth supports chewing, speech, and alignment — protecting your overall oral health.

Root canals don't have to hurt

Fear → Fact

"It will be unbearably painful." Today's techniques and anaesthesia mean most patients feel only mild pressure — many compare it to a filling.

Fear → Fact

"I'll be in pain for days after." Any tenderness is usually brief and manageable; your specialist will guide you through aftercare.

Fear → Fact

"Extraction is easier." Saving your tooth often provides better function, aesthetics, and long-term outcomes than removing it.

Services

Evidence-based endodontic care — from routine root canals to complex retreatment — delivered with microscopic precision.

Root Canal Treatment

Single- and multi-visit options tailored to your tooth. Thorough cleaning, shaping, and sealing for lasting relief.

Microscopic Endodontics

Magnification-assisted treatment for intricate anatomy — improved visibility means more predictable results.

Endodontic Retreatment

Careful revision of previously treated teeth — addressing persistent infection or incomplete healing.

Cracked Tooth Treatment

Assessment of crack depth and symptoms — stabilising the tooth and planning definitive endodontic care.

Dental Trauma Management

Urgent, protocol-driven care for injured teeth — aiming to preserve vitality and long-term prognosis.

Pulp Capping & Preservation

When appropriate, conservative approaches to maintain pulp health and delay or avoid full root canal therapy.

Why a Specialist?

Endodontics is a distinct discipline — specialist care can make the difference in comfort, precision, and long-term success.

Advanced Training Beyond BDS/MDS

Dedicated endodontic expertise means deeper understanding of pulp biology, pain diagnosis, and complex canal systems.

Microscope-Assisted Precision

Enhanced vision supports conservative access, thorough disinfection, and meticulous obturation.

Higher Success Rates

Specialist protocols and equipment are associated with predictable healing and fewer retreatments.

Complex Cases Welcome

Calcified canals, curved roots, resorption, and prior failed treatment — managed with calm, methodical skill.
